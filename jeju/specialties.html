제주 특산물 · 향토음식

Know Your Jeju
Food

제주 음식 — 먹기 전에 알아두기

Twelve essential dishes that define Jeju's culinary identity — from volcanic-soil black pork to haenyeo-caught sea urchin, from dialect-named porridge to brown-seaweed hangover stew. Know what to order, how to eat it, and where to find the best version.

01

흑돼지

Jeju Black Pork

Jeju's most iconic dish. Indigenous black pigs raised on volcanic soil. Chewier texture and richer savoury flavour than regular pork. Traditionally grilled over charcoal and dipped in 멜젓 (anchovy sauce), which cuts through the fat and creates an addictive loop.

Eat at 돈사촌 (원조 근고기), 돈사돈, 고향흑돼지, 옛날옛적
02

고기국수

Meat Noodle Soup

Jeju's civic dish. Milky pork-bone broth topped with sliced boiled pork, served over thin wheat noodles. Diners add chili flakes, black pepper, and green chili to taste. Found everywhere, but the shops argue bitterly about who's best. Medium-thickness noodles (중면) are preferred.

Eat at 자매국수, 올래국수, 골막식당, 국수마당
03

갈치요리

Hairtail / Cutlassfish

Jeju hairtail is legendary — thick, tender, prepared five ways: 갈치구이 (grilled), 갈치조림 (braised in soy), 갈치회 (raw sashimi — surprisingly not fishy), 갈치국 (soup), and 갈치젓 (fermented). The braised version is "sweet, spicy, and addictive."

Eat at 덕승식당, 수희네 (Jeju hairtail specialists)
04

성게미역국

Sea Urchin & Seaweed Soup

A nourishing soup and hangover cure. In Jeju dialect, sea urchin is 구살 (gusal) — locals call this 구살국. Seaweed sautéed in sesame oil, then sea urchin and 오분자기 (disk abalone) added and boiled. Warming, oceanic, restorative.

Eat at 해녀의 집, traditional seafood restaurants
05

전복

Abalone

Jeju is one of Korea's major abalone producers — a "magical ingredient" that elevates any dish. Eaten as 전복회 (raw sashimi), 전복죽 (porridge), 전복 뚝배기 (earthenware pot), or in stews. The liver is particularly prized.

Eat at 해녀의 집, 순옥이네명가, 명진전복
06

오분자기

Disk Abalone (Tteokjogae)

Called 떡조개 ("rice cake clam") in Jeju. A mollusk similar to abalone but smaller. Jeju produces 70% of Korea's total harvest. Eaten grilled, in 된장찌개, as porridge, or fermented into 젓갈. Lives on rocks at ~20m depth.

Eat at 해녀 houses, traditional markets
07

물회

Cold Raw Seafood Soup

Thin-sliced raw fish and vegetables in a spicy, vinegary broth — served cold or at room temperature. Best in spring (April–May). Varieties include sea cucumber, conch, flounder, and bigfin reef squid. A refreshing, sharp contrast to Jeju's heavier pork dishes.

Eat at 팔도수산식당, 순옥이네명가
08

복지리

Pufferfish Stew with Milt

A Jeju hangover specialty. Pufferfish and its "애" (milt/soft roe — "a symbol of freshness") cooked at the table with water parsley, mushrooms, and bean sprouts. The milt is delicate and soft; the meat is firm and chewy. Dip in soy sauce with vinegar. Sells out by noon.

Eat at 몰질 식육식당 (the specialist)
09

고등어회

Fresh Mackerel Sashimi

A rare delicacy "not easily found even for Jeju residents." Freshly caught mackerel served raw — contrary to expectations, it has no fishy smell at all when this fresh. Tender texture. Eaten with pickled onions and seaweed. Ends with 지리탕 (spicy bone stew).

Eat at 자연수산, 블랙씨걸
10

몸국

Brown Seaweed Soup (Momguk)

A Jeju 향토음식 (heritage dish). 몸 is Jeju dialect for 모자반 (stringy brown seaweed / mozuku). Has a distinctive natural odor that each restaurant manages through their own recipe. Excellent as both a meal and a 해장 (hangover cure). Featured in virtually every Jeju travel pamphlet.

Eat at 국수마당, 제주미담, traditional restaurants island-wide
11

빙떡

Rolled Buckwheat Rice Cake

Name means "rolled round and round" (빙빙 말아서). Buckwheat-flour crepe spread thin, filled with shredded radish, carrot, green onion, sesame powder, and sesame oil, then rolled into a cylinder. A traditional Jeju rice cake distinct from 오메기떡. Found at traditional markets.

Eat at 제주 전통시장 (Jeju traditional markets)
12

문게죽

Octopus Porridge

A rustic nourishing dish. 문게 (munge) is Jeju dialect for octopus — specifically 돌문어 (stone octopus). The octopus is pounded in a mortar, stir-fried in sesame oil with soaked rice, then boiled into porridge. Known for an exceptionally chewy texture from the pounded octopus.

Eat at 문게죽 전문점 (specialty restaurants)
13

보말죽

Sea Snail Porridge

보말 is Jeju dialect for 고둥 (sea conch/whelk). A savory porridge with a pumpkin-like color and slightly bitter edge. A heritage food that Matjondi reimagines for modern palates. Best in summer when the snails are at their peak.

Eat at 맛존디 본점
14

옥돔

Tilefish

Jeju's premium fish — soft, savory flesh, excellent grilled or as porridge. Often served as a complimentary side dish (the grilled version at Paldo Susikdang is a good example). A mark of quality at any seafood restaurant.

Eat at 삼대국수회관, 서문옥돔
15

꿩고기

Pheasant

Jeju has an abundance of pheasants; autumn pheasant (가을꿩) is finest. Eaten as sashimi, jerky, shabu-shabu (breast meat only), 꿩메밀국수 (buckwheat noodles in pheasant broth — "superior in both digestibility and flavour"), and 꿩 만두국 (dumpling soup). Niche but exceptional.

Eat at 꿩요리 전문점 (specialty pheasant restaurants)
16

돔베고기

Dombe Pork (Sliced Boiled Pork)

Jeju's answer to boiled pork (수육) — served on a cutting board (돔베 is dialect for 도마, cutting board). Thick slices of boiled black pork, eaten with Jeju kimchi and 멜젓. Often ordered alongside gogi-guksu. The purity of this preparation — just pork, boiled — is the test of the animal.

Eat at 자매국수, 청송 고기국수, any gogi-guksu shop
제주어 · Jeju Dialect

Speak Before You Eat 제주어 사전

Jeju dialect (제주어) is so distinct it's classified as a separate language. Knowing a few food words will earn you instant respect — and help you read menus that mainlanders can't.

멜젓

Meljeot — Anchovy Sauce

The essential black-pork dip. Fermented anchovy sauce — saltier and fishier than ssamjang, which is why locals use it. Cuts through pork fat like acid.

보말

Bomal — Sea Conch

Standard Korean: 고둥. The key ingredient in 보말죽. Best in summer. The word itself sounds like what it tastes like: briny, rounded, oceanic.

구살

Gusal — Sea Urchin

Standard Korean: 성게. When locals say 구살국, they mean sea urchin seaweed soup — the ultimate warming, restorative bowl.

문게

Munge — Octopus

Specifically 돌문어 (stone octopus). Pounded in a mortar for porridge (문게죽). The texture is unlike any octopus preparation you've had.

몸

Mom — Brown Seaweed

Standard Korean: 모자반. The key ingredient in 몸국 (momguk). Each restaurant has its own technique for managing the seaweed's natural odor.

돔베

Dombe — Cutting Board

Standard Korean: 도마. 돔베고기 = boiled pork served on a board. The name tells you everything: simple, direct, no pretense.

국시

Guksi — Noodles

An old Jeju word for noodles. You'll see it in restaurant names like 국시트멍 (Guksiteumeong) — literally "noodle clearing/passageway."

애

Ae — Milt / Soft Roe

As in the pufferfish milt in 복지리. Considered "a symbol of freshness" — only available when the pufferfish is at peak quality.
