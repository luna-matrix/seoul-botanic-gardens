Jeju City Eats

제주시 — 공항에서 도보권, 현지인이 먹는 곳

Thirteen restaurants within a 2km radius of the city core. This is your guide for the day before you hit the road and the day you come back — from the legendary gogi-guksu noodle wars to port-side sashimi at dawn and the black pork institution that needs no introduction.

팔도수산식당Paldo Susikdang

₩10,000–15,000
Sashimi & Raw Seafood Soup · 한치물회 · 회덮밥

The moment you land. Opens at 7:00 AM — practically dawn — near the western harbor breakwater. Their 한치물회 (swordfish cold raw-fish soup, ₩15,000) and 회덮밥 (sashimi rice bowl, ₩10,000) are a shockingly good way to start a Jeju trip. The complimentary 옥돔구이 (grilled tilefish) that arrives unbidden is a tell: this place is generous.

Why so early? The harbor is right there. Fish comes in at dawn; the kitchen serves it immediately. Not yet widely known — "highly recommended for mulhoe lovers," as the blogger who found it wrote, slightly amazed at the quality for the price.
제주시 임항로 27 · 15 min drive from airport Daily 07:00–23:00 Phone: 064-725-1181 · Parking: 3–4 spaces

신해바다Sinhaebada

₩8,000–12,000
Hangover Soup & Jeju Rice Meals · 해장국 · 백반

The other early-morning play. Sinhaebada serves 객주리조림 (braised filefish) — described as "clear and refreshing" — alongside proper hangover soup and Jeju-style 백반 set meals. Opens early, solo-dining friendly, and the best value is ordering "오늘의 메뉴" (today's menu) for a rotating plate of whatever's fresh.

Local trick: Ask if they have 자리돔젓 (rabbitfish jeotgal) or 깅이젓 (crab jeotgal) — Jeju's fermented seafood condiments that mainlanders rarely encounter. If available, spoon it over hot rice.
제주시 · Near Jeju Airport Opens early morning · Solo dining (혼밥) OK

남양해장국Namyang Haejangguk

₩8,000–14,000
Pork Bone & Abalone Hangover Soup · 뼈다귀해장국 · 전복해장국

The reliable neighbourhood anchor in Nohyeong-dong. Their 뼈다귀 해장국 (pork bone hangover soup) is generous and cheap; the 전복해장국 (abalone hangover soup) elevates the genre. Opens at 7:00 AM — ideal for a pre-itinerary meal that won't slow you down.

The Noodle Wars고기국수 전쟁

Gogi-guksu (고기국수) — pork-bone broth noodles topped with sliced boiled pork — is Jeju's civic dish. You'll find it everywhere, but these six shops are the ones locals argue about. Plan at least one bowl per day.

1
Pillar

자매국수Jamae Guksu

₩9,000
Gogi-Guksu · 고기국수

One of the "two great pillars" (양대산맥) of Jeju gogi-guksu. So popular with a single dish that they built a new building to accommodate the crowds. Known for its mild, clean "original" broth — a gentle introduction to the genre. Also serves bibim guksu, domebegi (pork), and unique special dumplings. The ticket-system entrance keeps the line moving.

The rivalry: Jeju locals split into Team Jamae and Team Ollae (below). Jamae's broth is milder and more approachable; Ollae's is deeper and milkier. Try both and pick a side — this is a conversation starter with any Jeju resident.
제주시 향골남길 46 09:00–18:00 · Closed Wed · Break 14:20–16:00 Ticket system · comparatively easy parking
2
Pillar

올래국수Ollae Guksu

₩9,000
Gogi-Guksu · 고기국수 (single menu)

The other pillar. Ollae serves one dish and one dish only: gogi-guksu. No bibim, no sides, no compromise. The broth is milky-white, clear, deep — simple but with a gravity that keeps people coming back. Always crowded; no dedicated parking lot (use nearby paid lots).

Tip: Drop off passengers first to save waiting time — the line moves fast but the parking situation is tight. Come before 11:00 or after 14:00 to avoid the longest queues.
제주시 귀아랑길 24 08:30–15:00 · Closed Sundays No parking lot — use nearby paid lots
★

골막식당Golmak Sikdang

₩8,000–12,000
Gogi-Guksu · 고기국수 · 수육

The self-declared "original" (원조) Jeju meat noodle restaurant. Featured on Running Man, visited by comedian Kim Jun-hyun, and appeared on Baek Jong-won's 3 Great Heavens. A local told a visiting blogger: "You're from the mainland, right? This place is the original meat noodle spot. You came to the right place."

First meal after landing: Opens at 06:30 — earlier than almost anywhere else — making it the perfect first bowl straight off the plane. Near the airport in Ido 2-dong. Also serves 수육 (boiled pork slices) as a side.
제주시 이도2동 362-1 · Near Jeju Airport 06:30–18:00 · Closed Sundays

국시트멍Guksiteumeong

₩9,000
Gogi-Guksu & Cold Noodles · 고기국수 · 냉면

The name combines guksi (old Jeju word for noodles) with teumeong, meaning "clearing a narrow passage." Started in 2013 as a tiny button shop in Nohyeong-dong. The noodles are thick and plump, the pork thinly sliced and wide with a special sauce. Halla University students line up here before national exams — a good sign.

Beyond gogi-guksu: Their 제주냉면 산도롱한면 (Jeju cold noodles, ₩9,000) is a sleeper hit — order it if you're noodled-out on the hot version.
제주시 진군길 31-3 · Near Nohyeong Park Weekdays 08:00–16:00 · Weekends 11:00–16:00 · LO 15:30
m
Local

국수마당Guksumadang

₩8,000–9,000
Gogi-Guksu & Momguk · 고기국수 · 몸국

The first meat noodle restaurant on Jeju Noodle Street (국수거리, Samseong-ro). Open until 2:00 AM — the late-night saviour. Broth is clean and mild, no pork odor or gaminess. Topped with dombe (a local dried radish/pork preparation), carrots, chili powder, green onions. Also serves 몸국 (momguk, Jeju brown-seaweed soup, ₩9,000).

Noodle Day: On the 11th of every month ("Noodle Day"), all noodle dishes are ₩500 off. A charming quirk that keeps regulars loyal.
제주시 삼성로 65 · Jeju Noodle Street 08:00–02:00 daily · ₩500 off on the 11th

Local

제주미담Jeju Midam

₩7,000–8,500
Gogi-Guksu, Momguk & Sundae · 고기국수 · 몸국 · 순대국

The generous one. Jeju Midam tops its gogi-guksu with reportedly 3× more pork than standard bowls — "almost an entire strip of pork sliced on top," as one review put it. Also famous for 막창순대 (makchang sundae — pork intestines stuffed with blood-sausage casing), served in heaping portions.

Value play: At ₩8,500 for gogi-guksu with triple pork, this is one of the best price-to-quantity ratios on the island. Leftovers can be packed to go — locals do exactly that.
제주시 가령로 19 09:00–22:00 · Closed Tuesdays Also serves: 멸치국수 ₩7,000 · 몸국 ₩8,500 · 순대국밥 ₩8,000

Local

돈사돈 본점Donsadon — Main Branch

₩15,000–25,000
Charcoal Black Pork BBQ · 숯불 흑돼지

The go-to for proper Jeju black pork in the city centre, in Yeon-dong. Charcoal-grilled thick-cut neck (목살) with a fat-to-lean balance that hits from the first bite. Reservation essential. Note: you grill it yourself here — no staff grilling service. Come confident, or come with someone who is.

Dip correctly: The traditional Jeju way is 멜젓 (멸치젓, anchovy sauce) — not ssamjang. The salt-umami of the anchovy cuts through the pork fat and creates an addictive loop. Some places also offer 기름장 (oil-salt mixture) as an alternative.
제주시 연동 · Central Jeju City Reservation essential · Self-grill Recently added self-service bar

만강촌옛날칼국수Mangangchon Kalguksu

₩8,000–12,000
Half-Chicken Knife Noodles · 닭반마리 칼국수

Not noodles in the gogi-guksu sense — this is a different beast. A half chicken arrives submerged in knife-cut noodle soup. You grab the chicken leg, tear the meat, eat it with noodles and kimchi together — "feels like eating samgyetang for nourishment," as the Visit Jeju write-up put it. Designated a 착한가격업소 (honest-price business). Unlimited noodle and rice refills.

Bonus: They sell 옛날 통닭 (old-style fried chicken) for takeaway — excellent late-night food. Handmade dumplings (수제 만두) available in single portions too. 10–15 minutes from the airport.
제주시 월랑로 42 · 10–15 min from airport 11:00–21:30 daily · LO 21:00 Unlimited noodle refills · Phone: 064-744-4780

순옥이네명가 본점Sunok's House — Main Branch

₩10,000–16,000
Grilled Mackerel & Water Sashimi · 고등어구이 · 물회

Perfect for a post-sunset dinner after walking Olle Trail Course 17 along the coast. 고등어구이 (grilled mackerel, ₩10,000) comes in large portions with lightly seasoned side dishes. The house 물회 (water sashimi, ₩16,000) is a generous raw-seafood soup. Spacious interior, good for groups.

The walk: Stroll from Iho Breakwater (이호방파제) past Dodubong Peak — the coastal approach is part of the experience. Nearby Dodu Harbor makes this a natural evening combo: sunset walk, then grilled mackerel.
제주시 도공로 8 · Near Dodu Harbor / Olle Trail 17 09:00–21:00 · LO 20:30 · Break 15:30–17:00 Closed 2nd & 4th Tuesdays · Phone: 064-784-4813

모던 돔베Modern Dombae

₩13,000
Upscale Gogi-Guksu · 고기국수 (파인 다이닝)

The fine-dining gogi-guksu. Presentation resembles Japanese chashu ramen — thick, long-sliced meat draped over noodles like an art piece. Uses homemade soba (buckwheat) noodles for a unique aroma. Topped with charcoal-grilled Jeju black pork belly. The priciest bowl on the island at ₩13,000, but reviewers call it 가심비 (worth the psychological satisfaction).

Why it matters: This is what happens when a chef takes Jeju's humblest dish and treats it with precision. If you think you've exhausted gogi-guksu, this reframes it entirely. The soba noodle aroma changes the whole experience.
제주시 1100로 3029 11:00–20:00 · Break 16:30–17:30
