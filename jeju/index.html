The Premise · 시작하며

For the Foodie Who's Done the Tourist Hits 관광객 맛집은 그만

You've been to Jeju. You've done the Heukdonga row in Jeju City, eaten at the famous pork belly place near the airport, queued for tangerine ice cream on Udo. This guide is for what comes next.

Built entirely from Korean-language food blogs, Naver reviews, 다이닝코드 ratings, and 비짓제주's local-verified lists, this atlas charts the restaurants where Jeju Island residents actually eat — the haenyeo-house with no sign, the noodle shop that built a new building for its queue, the pufferfish stew that sells out by noon.

The guide is split into three sections: Jeju City centre for your arrival and departure days, the island-wide atlas for your 3–4 day road trip (organised by coast), and a specialties reference for the 12+ dishes you can't leave without trying. Every restaurant has an interactive map pin and one-click Naver Maps / Google links.
